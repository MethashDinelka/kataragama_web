Visit Kataragama

Kataragama is a sacred pilgrimage town in Sri Lanka, revered by Hindus, Buddhists, Muslims, and the indigenous Vedda people. It's located in the Monaragala District of Uva province, about 228 km southeast of Colombo. The town is known for its Kataragama temple, dedicated to Skanda Kumara (also known as Kataragama deviyo), and the Kiri Vehera Buddhist stupa. Kataragama is a fast-developing township surrounded by jungle, and it was historically the seat of government for Sinhalese kings.
